## Omissions (cross-referenced against HFC-based SOPs)

### Significant Gaps

1. **Emergency contacts / phone numbers** — No section for emergency numbers (100 Police, 101 MDA, 102 Fire, 104 HFC). The SOPs repo has a full SOP-12 on this. A printed emergency contacts list is mentioned nowhere in the checklist.

2. **Family emergency plan / roles** — No mention of assigned roles (who grabs baby, who grabs go bag, who closes doors/windows), meeting places, or the order of actions (Notify → Shut off → Distance → Close → Act → Listen). SOP-10 covers this comprehensively.

3. **Mamad inspection / home preparation** — The checklist says "☐ PREPARED" for the Mamad but doesn't cover: blast door seal integrity, rubber insulation, steel/glass window function, ventilation pipes, flammable/hazardous materials check, securing heavy furniture, gas/electricity shutoff knowledge. SOP-11 covers all of this.

4. **Siren response procedures** — No section on what to do when the siren actually sounds (by scenario: at home with/without Mamad, driving, on bus, outside, in elevator, in a public building, in a prefab). The "wait 10 minutes" rule is not mentioned. SOP-6/SOP-8 cover this.

5. **Protected space priority order** — Mamad → Shelter → Inner stairwell → Inner room → Last resort (lie flat). Not referenced anywhere in the checklist.

6. **Evacuation route** — No mention of primary/secondary exit routes from home, or that all household members should know them.

7. **Gas/electricity shutoff** — Not mentioned. HFC guidance says all family members must know locations and how to operate them.

8. **Accessibility/disability equipment** — No mention of hearing aids, wheelchair supplies, guide dog gear, communication mediation tags, buddy system for mobility-limited persons. SOP-14 covers this.

9. **Pet preparedness** — No mention of pet food, carrier/crate, vet documents, shelter pet policy, thick socks for dogs. SOP-15 covers this.

10. **Spare keys** in go bag (house + car).

11. **Water in go bag** — The go bag section doesn't include water bottles in the essentials (only in the "overnight stays" extras).

12. **Printed emergency contacts list** — Mentioned zero times. HFC emphasises not relying on digital contacts.

13. **Medical status sheet** — Not mentioned (printed list of conditions, medications, allergies from family doctor).

14. **Car fuel level** — Escalation readiness (SOP-3) includes ensuring car has at least half a tank.

15. **Drills** — No mention of practice drills or timed runs to shelter.

16. **Maintenance schedule** — No mention of periodic resupply (water/food every 3 months, batteries every 6 months).

17. **Information discipline** — No mention of not spreading unverified information (covered in SOP-12).

18. **Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA)** — "Ensure 'Extreme' threats are alerting" is mentioned but there's no iOS equivalent guidance alongside the Android-focused Appendix 1.

### Minor Gaps

- No mention of glasses/contacts in personal readiness
- No mention of activities for children during shelter stays (in go bag or mamad)
- Emergency whistle is in "Additionals" but HFC considers it more essential
- No can opener mentioned with food supplies
- No change of clothes in go bag essentials
- No blanket/sleeping bag for shelter stays

## Proposed Quick/Essentials Smoke Test

A periodic rapid check (suggested mnemonic below) covering the absolute minimum readiness posture:

| # | Item | Check |
|---|------|-------|
| 1 | **P**hone | On, charged, HFC app running, correct area |
| 2 | **A**lerts | Wireless emergency alerts enabled, DND override verified |
| 3 | **W**ater | 72-hour supply accessible and not expired |
| 4 | **S**helter | Nearest 3 shelters known, routes walkable |
| 5 | **B**ag | Go bag by door, zipped, contents verified |
| 6 | **E**xit | Hallway clear, door unlockable quickly |
| 7 | **D**ependents | All household members present or accounted for |

**Mnemonic: PAWS BED** — "Check your PAWS before BED"

Suggested frequency: Daily during wartime/escalation, weekly during elevated tension, monthly during routine periods.

## Summary

The V1 checklist is a solid personal readiness document covering tech alerts, home environment, go bag, and personal effects well. The main gaps are in **response procedures** (what to do when the siren sounds), **family coordination** (roles, meeting places, drills), **home hardening** (mamad inspection, furniture securing, shutoffs), and **reference information** (emergency phone numbers, printed contacts). Adding these would make it a truly comprehensive unified master checklist aligned with HFC guidance.
